Clinical Process

Triage, Assessment & Tiering

Step-by-step process from first contact through tier assignment and referral initiation.

Step 1 — Immediate Safety Screen

If YES to any, do same-day escalation first, then return to triaging plan:

  • Immediate medical concern / needs ER
  • Acute intoxication/withdrawal risk needing medical review
  • Current risk of harm to self/others (or cannot care for self right now)
  • Severe confusion / disorientation

Action: Call appropriate crisis/medical response. Arrange transport. Document incident report + follow-up plan.

Step 2 — Consent Checkpoint

  • HART Hub written consent completed
  • Consent covers service coordination + referral sharing among HART Hub partners
  • Client preferences recorded (contact method, cultural supports, goals)

Step 3 — Triage Domains (Check + Connect)

For each domain: check need, then pick at least one connection.

  • Basic needs: Food, clothing, hygiene, laundry, transport, ID → Onsite support/navigation
  • Housing instability: Unsheltered, couch surfing, shelter use, eviction risk → Housing navigation, supportive housing pathway
  • Mental health: Distress impacting function, recurrent crisis/ED use → Crisis stabilization, partner MH pathway, ICM
  • Substance use: Wants help/change, harm concerns, overdose history → Addictions specialist, RAAM referral
  • Ongoing coordination: Multiple systems, frequent follow-up needed → HART Hub case management, KACL ICM
  • Cultural connection: Indigenous-led, Western clinical, or both → Plan reflects client goals (Two-Eyed Seeing)

Step 4 — Assign Service Tier

Use as staffing guidance, not eligibility. See Service Tiers tab for full detail.

Step 5 — Referral Actions

For each referral: document service, method (warm handoff/phone/email/fax/Care Team), consent shared, follow-up date, and outcome (accepted/pending/declined/redirected).

1Mild

Tier 1 — Mild

Peer support, transportation support, system navigation, cultural supports, Hub Coordinator, primary care. Meets immediate needs (food, hygiene, ID). Re-screen at next contact for emerging needs.

2Mod

Tier 2 — Moderate

All Tier 1 services plus case management and addiction specialist. Stabilize with onsite specialist first. RAAM only after assessment confirms medication need.

3Severe

Tier 3 — Severe

All Tier 1 + 2 services plus crisis stabilization and intensive case management. ICM is the anchor. Secondary referrals assigned through case conference / ICT daily huddle.

Safety Override Rule

Safety overrides everything. Crisis or medical emergency → 911 or ER immediately. All other referrals pause until safe.

Safety Screen Triggers

Immediate medical concern / needs ER? → Call EMS / 911
Acute intoxication/withdrawal needing medical review? → Call EMS / ER transfer
Risk of harm to self/others? → Crisis response + transport
Severe confusion / disorientation? → Medical review + incident report

After stabilization, return to triage plan. Document incident report and follow-up plan.

Pathways

Referral Matrix & Priority Rules

Sequential, not shotgun — one anchoring referral first, then build based on readiness and response.

1

Safety overrides everything. Crisis/medical emergency → 911 or ER immediately. All other referrals pause until safe.

2

One lead referral first. Identify the primary need and make one anchoring referral. Do not send multiple external referrals simultaneously.

3

Internal first, then partner. Use HART Hub onsite services before referring externally. Multiple internal Care Team referrals are fine.

4

Least intrusive, most culturally responsive first. If multiple options fit, choose the one closest to client preference and readiness.

5

Sequence by readiness. Second referral only when the first is accepted, active, or waitlisted. Third referral only after case conference or huddle.

6

Medical issues defer to primary care immediately. Client-requested cultural provider is prioritized unless unsafe.

Service Guide

Addiction Services

What HART Hub offers in scope, what requires external referral, and your decision guide for each situation.

HART Hub provides short-term, coordinated, low-barrier addiction support. Services focus on stabilization, engagement, and connection.

Engagement & Triage

Meet clients where they are. Identify immediate needs, safety concerns, and priorities. Obtain consent before referrals.

Brief Addiction Support

Motivational conversations. Substance use goal clarification. Group programming.

Crisis Stabilization

Support during intoxication, withdrawal concerns, or acute distress. Coordinate with crisis, detox, and emergency services when needed.

Care Coordination

Schedule and support referrals. Share information across services (with consent). Reduce missed appointments and drop-off.

Peer Support

Relationship-based engagement. Practical support navigating services.

Primary Care Linkage

Connect to primary care where available. Support medication continuity through case management.

Housing Navigation

Connect addiction needs with housing pathways. Support stabilization as part of housing readiness.

HART Hub does not replace specialized addiction services. Refer out when clients need:

Ongoing Addiction Treatment

Refer to: Del-Art, Migizi, LWDH MHA, RAAM
Long-term counselling, therapy, or structured substance use treatment programs.

Medical Detox / Withdrawal Management

Refer to: Morningstar
Moderate to severe withdrawal risk. Medical monitoring beyond brief stabilization.

Harm Reduction Services

Refer to: NWHU, RAAM
Needle exchange. Safer supply programs. Supervised consumption not available in Kenora region.

Inpatient / Live-In Treatment

Refer to: Del-Art, Migizi
Treatment beds. Live-in addiction programs.

RAAM / LWDH Addiction Services

Refer when clients need addiction medicine, medication support for substance use, or specialized assessment and treatment. Your role: Support referral, help client attend appointments, coordinate follow-up. Client needs ID for RAAM.

Emergency Department (Lake of the Woods)

Use ED when there is medical instability, severe withdrawal, overdose or high medical risk, or acute psychiatric risk. Your role: Use developed pathways when transferring. Obtain discharge info, document in client file, alert circle of care.

Community Addiction Agencies (Del-Art, Migizi)

Refer when clients need ongoing counselling, group programming, or culturally specific/community-based supports. Your role: Match service to client readiness and preference. Reduce barriers (transport, reminders, accompaniment).

Withdrawal Management / Detox (Morningstar)

Refer when clients need medically supervised withdrawal or short-term stabilization prior to treatment. Your role: Prepare client, coordinate timing, support transition after discharge.

Housing + Addiction Pathways (WNHAC, KDSB)

Addiction support often intersects with housing. You can coordinate with supportive housing providers, connect clients to rent supplements, and support transitions between shelter, housing, and services.

Quick Decision Guide for Staff

Brief support + coordination needed? → HART Hub in scope → Use CARE TEAM
Medication or addiction specialist needed? → Refer to RAAM / LWDH MHA → Interagency Form
Medical risk or severe withdrawal? → Emergency Department → Utilize EMS
Ongoing counselling or treatment? → Community agency → Appropriate referral pathway
Harm reduction services? → External program → Connect with NWHU

Your Role Matters

You are not expected to “fix” addiction. You create access, reduce barriers, and hold the connection while clients move through the system and stages. Start with engagement. Stabilize what you can. Loop in support. Refer early rather than late.

Housing

Supportive Housing

Supportive housing is a core outcome of the HART Hub model — not an adjunct service.

Supportive Housing as an Integrated System Function

  • Coordinated Access and prioritization (KDSB-led)
  • Shared referral pathways
  • Integrated case management
  • Ongoing data-sharing and performance monitoring

What Clients Typically Require

  • Ongoing outreach and engagement
  • Community-based service delivery (not office-based)
  • Coordinated care planning across housing, health, and addiction
  • Flexible response to fluctuating needs

Staff should expect continued involvement after housing placement, not discharge at placement.

Reasons for Referral (Prioritization)

  • Repeated shelter use or unsheltered homelessness
  • High service utilization across health, shelter, or justice
  • Inability to maintain housing without ongoing support
  • Identified need for integrated MH, addiction, and housing support

Monitoring & Outcomes (System-Level)

  • Housing retention (12+ months)
  • Reduced emergency shelter use
  • Reduced hospital and ED utilization
  • Improved functional and wellness outcomes
  • Decreased chronic homelessness rates

Case Management Services

Overview of case management and service navigation across partner agencies.

Service Navigation

  • Service Navigator offered through the Clinical Service Hub
  • SOLGEN partnership: part-time navigator for individuals on community supervision orders (contract ends March 31, 2026)

Case Management Programs

  • Assertive Community Treatment (ACT): For eligible individuals with specific criteria
  • Forensic Case Manager: For individuals in Kenora with active criminal charges not attached to a current provider
  • Mobile MH&A Clinic Case Manager: Serves 5 remote communities as part of multi-disciplinary team. Landing site at Main Office.
  • Housing Case Manager: Supports residents in Kenora Supportive Housing (2 days/week) and Safe Bed Program (3 days/week). Works with existing community case managers.
  • Diversion Worker: At Clinical Service Hub, serves clients flagged as chronically homeless (14+ days in shelter/month). Assists with housing applications and 3-month transition support.

Intensive Case Management

Support for adults 18+ who identify with a serious mental illness. Direct referrals to the program. Participation is voluntary.

Adult Protective Services

Supports for adults 18+ with a diagnosed developmental disability. Accessed through Developmental Services Ontario (DSO) and deemed eligible through DSO.

Options for Adults

Varied levels of supports for adults 18+ with a diagnosed developmental disability. Accessed through DSO, deemed eligible, and matched to residential support.

KCA Programs

  • Adult MH: Case management for members of Treaty 3 First Nation communities
  • CYMH: Child and Youth Mental Health services
  • YOW: Youth Outreach Worker program
  • YIT (16–25): Youth in Transition services
Documentation

Forms & Consent

Key documentation templates for client intake, consent, and information sharing.

HART Hub Consent for Services + Information Sharing

Client Info

Legal name, preferred name, DOB, phone (can we leave a message?), preferred contact method (call / text / email / other).

What I Am Consenting To

HART Hub services and supports which may include: case management/transition support, addictions supports, primary care, peer support, housing navigation, crisis stabilization, transportation support, and basic needs support.

Information Sharing

Consent to share relevant information with HART Hub funded partners (for coordination) and non-funded partners (only when needed for referral). Information limited to what is needed to connect to services, coordinate care, and reduce repeating story.

Client Choices

  • Can use overnight shelter without HART Hub services
  • Can refuse any service or referral
  • Can withdraw consent at any time (verbally or in writing)

Limits

Staff may need to share information without consent if there is a serious safety concern or a legal requirement.

What Can Be Shared (check all that apply)

  • Contact info
  • Housing situation
  • Health / mental health needs (general)
  • Substance use needs (general)
  • Service history and current supports
  • Safety concerns relevant to service planning
  • Cultural/community supports wanted

Care Team Platform

HART Hub-funded staff may document service information in Care Team for internal coordination. This is separate from external referral documentation.

Referral Methods

  • Internal (Care Team): Multiple onsite referrals encouraged
  • Warm Handoff: For urgent crisis or stabilization referrals
  • Interagency Referral Form: For RAAM, LWDH, and external partners
  • SBAR: Used for transfers from Morningstar or ER
  • KDSB Coordinated Access: For supportive housing pathways

48-Hour Referral Outcome Loop

Outcome reported back to referring agency within 48 hours: Accepted / Pending / Declined by Client / Redirected by Service.
